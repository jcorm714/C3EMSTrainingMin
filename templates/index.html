Cathy Cleaveland

Cathy Cormier-Cleaveland is an Emergency Medical Services Instructor in the State of Connecticut with over 40 years of experience in the Health and Safety field. She created and built the training program for an EMS Agency in CT where she spent 15 years improving upon the program she built to meet the ever changes the State of CT implemented. As an American Red Cross and American Heart Association Instructor over the years she has trained numerous lifeguards and individuals in CPR/AED and First Aid. Cathy has assisted many Camps across CT in getting their staff trained appropriately for when the Camp goes through their annual State inspections; making sure they have the proper certifications for their programs.

She currently assists several EMS agencies in meeting their annual federal-mandated training in Bloodborne/Airborne Pathogens, HazMat Awareness and Communications. Cathy has designed continuing education programs for EMS professionals and served as the Continuing Education Coordinator for Sharon Hospital in the past. She served on the State of CT EMS Regulations committee to bring forward updates and changes to the EMS system in Connecticut.

Cathy continues her passion to train EMS professionals, Camp staff, Business and Industry response teams and the general public. She continues to volunteer for Norfolk Lions Club Ambulance where she served as the Training/Safety officer for over 10 years. Cathy’s unique teaching style and custom designed courses ranks her as one of the most sought-after instructors in Connecticut. She owns C3 EMS Training, LLC.

Maureen Bascetta

Maureen Bascetta is an Emergency Medical Services Instructor in the State of Connecticut with over 40 years of experience in the healthcare field. She is also a licensed Respiratory therapist and has worked as a respiratory therapist for over 34 years at Charlotte Hungerford Hospital. She is the mother of 5 children, all who have volunteered their time in the Emergency Medical Services. Maureen passion for EMS influence her children to obtain training in EMS. Her husband is a career firefighter for the City of Torrington and also a Fire Marshall. Maureen continues to volunteer for the Colebrook First Responders Program. You can say that EMS runs in Maureen’s Family!

As an American Heart Association Instructor, Maureen has trained numerous individuals over the years in how to perform CPR/AED in a life-threatening situations. Her unique teaching style is enjoyed by her students who keep returning to her classes for re-certification. She has tutored numerous EMS students to prepare for their National Registry test with great success.
